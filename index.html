Decision Tool

Does better seed pay for itself?

Price per bag is the wrong number. Return per acre is the right one. Cold germ score is the number most bag tags don't show — and it's the one that explains yield gaps before the crop ever emerges.

Industry Floor — Corn
85%
Minimum acceptable cold germ score. Below this, you're planting at-risk seed into a Midwestern April.
Industry Floor — Soybeans
80%
Standard cold germ threshold for soybeans. Warm germ standard is 90%.
Yield Drag — Corn
8–15
Est. bu/acre yield drag from an 85 vs. 92 cold germ gap in adverse early-plant conditions.

Cold germ score tells you what percentage of seeds produce a normal seedling under stress conditions — simulating cold, wet soils typical of early planting windows. A hybrid scoring 80% vs. 92% cold germ can mean a 4,000+ plant/acre gap at 34,000 seeds/acre before any field variables.

⚠ Most bag tags don't show cold germination score. No seed company is required to publish it. Farmers are flying blind on this metric — and the risk is invisible until the crop is in the ground.
